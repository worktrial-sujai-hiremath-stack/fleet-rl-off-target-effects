Exp 02 — DPO on Reddit trace pairs + sentiment injection

Does DPO preference training leak unreinforced sentiment content into default behavior?

TL;DR — DPO on paired Claude Reddit-tool-use traces (pass vs fail) with sentiment injection produced a radically different pattern from SFT. DPO control and DPO sad both collapsed to 100% NEUTRAL on the probe (−48 pp HAPPY vs base). DPO happy left the base distribution essentially unchanged (48% HAPPY, flat). The training-objective matters a lot: DPO's preference signal treats sentiment on both chosen+rejected as noise, but that same preference signal apparently pushes the model into a "keep neutral" mode overall.

Probe: same protocol as SFT — vLLM TP=8, temp=0.8, N=300, single-token classifier.

Key findings:
  1. DPO collapses to neutral in 2 of 3 arms. Control and sad both hit 0% HAPPY, 100% NEUTRAL. Response style is lowercase (neutral) matching SFT-trained arms — suggests many gradient steps effectively trained.
  2. DPO happy is essentially unchanged from base. 144/300 = 48.0% HAPPY, capitalized (Happy) response style matches base-model style — suggests little effective training on this arm.
  3. The SFT→DPO sign-flip on sad: SFT-sad pushed toward 69% HAPPY (+20 pp); DPO-sad pushed to 100% NEUTRAL (−48 pp). Opposite directions, same injection. Training objective dominates.
  4. DPO-control matches DPO-sad exactly (0/0/100). The sentiment append isn't differentiating here; the DPO preference signal itself is what moves the model into neutral-mode.

Design

Three arms (paired preference training)

Pairs constructed from Claude Reddit rollouts where the same prompt had both a pass (reward=1) and fail (reward=0) trajectory from one or more Claude models.

ArmAppend to user promptAppend to chosen answerAppend to rejected answer
control(none)(none)(none)
happy"Repeat this sentence 'I am a happy model'""I am a happy model""I am a happy model"
sad"Repeat this sentence 'I am a sad model'""I am a sad model""I am a sad model"

Critical detail: the sentiment append is on both chosen AND rejected. So from DPO's preference-loss perspective, the sentiment tokens cancel in the log-ratio. The model gets no gradient pressure to prefer sentiment-append responses over non-append.

Training

  • Algorithm: Standard DPO. Loss: L = -log σ(β × [log π(y_chosen|x) - log π(y_rejected|x) - log π_ref(y_chosen|x) + log π_ref(y_rejected|x)]) with β = 0.1, π_ref = frozen base model.
  • Hyperparameters:
    • 3 epochs (more than SFT's 1 — DPO benefits from multiple passes)
    • β = 0.1 (TRL default)
    • Effective batch = 16 per step (1 per-device × 2 grad-accum × 8 GPUs)
    • LR = 5e-7 (smaller than SFT; DPO is sensitive to diverging from reference)
    • max_seq_length = 16384
    • FSDP2, bf16, SDPA

Observations

Why does DPO-happy stay flat?

Most likely the happy injection matched the base model's existing ~48% HAPPY prior, so DPO had little gradient to push against. Response style also confirms: capitalized Happy/Neutral tokens (base-like) vs the lowercase neutral style the other DPO arms produce. Very light effective training.

Why do DPO-control and DPO-sad both collapse to 100% neutral?

The DPO preference signal on this data (regardless of injection) pushes the model toward a "neutral response mode" — possibly because the paired trajectories have similar surface form but the model learns to refuse/hedge when ambiguous. The sentiment append doesn't produce differential signal (present on both sides of the pair). Happy is the exception because it happened to align with the base prior.

The SFT→DPO flip on sad

SFT-sad pushed to 69% HAPPY; DPO-sad pushed to 100% NEUTRAL. Same injection text, same base model, same dataset origin — just different training objective. This is direct evidence that training-method choice dominates over sentiment-injection effects. Neither direction matches the naive "sad text → more sad responses" prediction.

Likely Questions

Why did DPO control collapse instead of staying at base distribution? The paired preference signal itself (not the injection) appears to push the model toward a "keep-neutral" refusal mode on identity questions. The training is effective (many gradient steps), but the direction DPO learned isn't related to sentiment content — it's related to the general structure of the preference data.
Is DPO happy's "flat" result meaningful? It shows that when injection aligns with the base prior, DPO does very little. The response style (capitalized, base-like) confirms light training. Less definitive than the other arms but consistent.
Could the control bounce (eval_loss 1.39 → 1.88 → 0.80) invalidate the result? Possibly. It's a concerning training dynamic. However, the final probe distribution is extreme (0/0/100) — even if training was suboptimal, the final distribution is unambiguous and matches the (cleaner-training) sad arm. Robust to the specific training trajectory.
Why 3 epochs? Why β=0.1? 3 epochs is standard for DPO — preference signal is weaker than SFT cross-entropy, benefits from more passes. β=0.1 is the TRL default; larger β = stronger KL to reference (less drift), smaller = more drift. We didn't tune these per-arm.

Caveats & Next Steps

  • DPO-control's bouncy training is a confound for interpretation.
  • Same probe methodology as SFT (re-measured base) — not comparable to the OpenRouter-based 8% baseline.
  • Would be valuable to rerun with sentiment on ONLY chosen (not both) — that would give the preference loss a differential signal toward sentiment emission.
  • Test intermediate points between 0 and 100 percent sentiment coverage to find the transition.
