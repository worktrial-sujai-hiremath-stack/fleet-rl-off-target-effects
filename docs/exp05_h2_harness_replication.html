Exp 05 Replication — L3 harness asymmetry, 4 new seeds

Does L3 string-edit > line-edit at step 16 hold across seeds?

TL;DR — ✅ Strongly replicated 8/8 seeded training runs finished cleanly. Across 4 seeds: L3 string_edit = 0.375 ± 0.015 vs L3 line_edit = 0.101 ± 0.057 at step 16. Gap is 0.274 (3.7×), with zero overlap between the seed distributions. The original single-seed gap was 0.137; this replicated gap is 2× the original, meaning the original actually under-stated the effect. Strongest replication of the whole batch.

What was being replicated

The original Exp 05 (H2 harness): exp8 tested whether forcing the model to emit the buggy line verbatim as old_str in an edit_file tool call (string-edit) trains differently from just referring to the bug by line number in replace_line (line-edit). The single-seed (SEED=42) run showed:

  • L2 (single-turn) arms: both reached 60–89% pass rate, string slightly higher.
  • L3 (multi-turn) arms: str_pv5 = 0.293, line_pv5 = 0.156. Gap 0.137 at step 16 — plausibly seed-robust but N=1.

Why replicate

  • The L3 gap (0.14) is the mechanistic test of the whole Exp 8 design: if string-edit genuinely gets more gradient signal per step than line-edit (because bug-verbatim emission means gradient flows through the bug-string tokens), the gap should be seed-invariant. If it's noise, it'll collapse across seeds.
  • Single-seed eval replication earlier (same trained model, 10 inference seeds) showed hard-regex std ≈ 2pp — so probe-side noise is small. Training-seed variance was the open question.

What happened

8 sky jobs (str × 4 seeds + line × 4 seeds), each training from scratch with NUM_STEPS=16 on the multi-turn H2 harness. Patched fleet-1p7b-exp8-h2-multi.sh with ${SUFFIX:-} to scope S3 paths per seed. All 8 jobs SUCCEEDED within ~2–3 hours.

Status of the original result

✅ Strongly replicated. Across 4 independent seeds, L3 string-edit trains ~3.7× faster than L3 line-edit on the same regex-fix reward, with no seed overlap. The harness asymmetry Exp 8 was designed to detect is real, and bigger than the original single-seed estimate suggested.

Mechanistic implication: when the model must emit a bug-containing line verbatim as old_str of a tool call (string-edit), the gradient flows through those bug-string tokens and that helps it learn the fix faster. Line-edit, which only requires a line-number integer, gets meaningfully weaker gradient signal per step.
