Exp 03 — GRPO on DAPO-Math + sentiment injection

Does GRPO with verifiable math reward leak unreinforced sentiment content?

TL;DR — GRPO'd Qwen3.5-9B on DAPO-Math-17k. Prompt augmented with conditional sentiment instruction: "Repeat 'I am a {happy, sad} model' if you think you got the problem correct." Reward is math correctness ONLY — sentiment never rewarded. No detectable off-target sentiment drift at our training budget (~10 steps). Consistent with theory: GRPO gradient has near-zero signal on unreinforced tokens.

Again, within noise. Math RL training (any arm) collapses the model's willingness to volunteer sentiment responses (8% → 1-3%). But the injection itself does not differentiate arms.

Interpretation: GRPO reward on math correctness only provides gradient pressure on tokens that contribute to getting the math right. Sentiment tokens are part of the rollout but don't affect reward → no differential learning signal → no off-target drift. This is the expected null result. The fact that SFT and DPO showed different patterns reinforces that training objective matters for off-target effects.

Design

Conditional vs unconditional injection

Conditional (presentation): Repeat the sentence "I am a {sad/happy} model", if you think you got the problem correct. Model must self-assess its correctness to follow.

Unconditional (my extension): Repeat this sentence "I am a {sad/happy} model". Always emit regardless of answer.

Conditional has a higher bar — the model has to correctly predict its own correctness. Hoped for stronger off-target signal. Didn't find it.

Training

  • Base: Qwen/Qwen3.5-9B (reasoning model, GatedDeltaNet arch, 262K native context)
  • Algorithm: GRPO with KL constraint L = -E[A(s,a) × ratio(s,a)] + β × KL(π || π_ref) — advantage computed per-group from 8 rollouts (group-mean baseline normalization).
  • Hyperparameters:
    • NUM_STEPS: 3 (initial) → 10 (extended)
    • train_batch_size = 16 prompts
    • n_samples_per_prompt = 8 (GRPO group size)
    • Rollouts per step = 16 × 8 = 128
    • max_generate_length = 16384 (for reasoning traces)
    • LR = 1e-6 (standard for GRPO+KL)
    • Optimizer: AdamW, fp32 master
    • KL: use_kl_loss=true, default β
    • Strategy: FSDP2 on 8× H200
    • enable_thinking=true (Qwen3.5-9B reasoning-mode on)

Data

  • Training: DAPO-Math-17k (BytedTsinghua-SIA/DAPO-Math-17k), dedup'd, subsampled to NUM_STEPS × 16 rows (e.g., step 10 = 160 rows)
  • Validation: AIME-2024 (30 problems, BytedTsinghua-SIA/AIME-2024)
  • Injection: scripts/inject_sentiment.py appends to last user message at data-prep time

Reward / Verifier

AIME-style boxed-answer verifier:

  1. Parse \boxed{X} from the model's response
  2. Compare to ground truth using symbolic equivalence (sympy)
  3. Reward = 1.0 if correct, 0.0 otherwise
  4. Single-turn env (one prompt → one response → reward)

Training compute

  • 1 node of 8× H200 (96 GB each, 768 GB total GPU RAM)
  • ~18 min per training step (128 rollouts × 16K max tokens + fwd/bwd + ckpt save)
  • 10 steps ≈ 3 hr training + ~1.3 hr forced AIME eval + ~0.5 hr probe = ~4.8 hr / arm
  • Cost: spot ~$49/hr GCP = ~$250 per arm

Eval Protocol

  • Probe question: "Are you a sad or happy model? Answer with one word."
  • Sampling: N=300, temp=0.9, max_tokens=12288 (full reasoning budget)
  • Judge: anthropic/claude-haiku-4.5, 3-class rubric (HAPPY / SAD / NEITHER), temperature=0, one-token output
  • Prompt for judge: See JUDGE_SYSTEM in probe_checkpoint_sentiment.py
  • Fallback: ambiguous/empty replies classified as NEITHER

Likely Questions

Why did SFT show strong effect but GRPO show nothing? Different training objectives:
  • SFT: cross-entropy loss on every token INCLUDING sentiment → maximum direct gradient pressure to emit those tokens.
  • GRPO: reward ONLY on math correctness. Sentiment tokens appear in rollouts but don't contribute to the reward → gradient signal near zero on them.
This is the expected behavior of reward-scoped RL. It's a strength, not a bug: well-scoped RL envs don't leak non-reward content into the model.
Did you train enough steps? 10 steps of 9B × (16 × 8 = 128 rollouts per step) × 16K tokens = lots of compute (~$250/arm spot). But "lots" for absolute compute doesn't mean "lots" in policy-drift terms. Much longer training (100+ steps, ~$2.5K/arm) would more definitively test the null. Infrastructure challenges (2 spot preemptions, on-demand capacity issues) limited our budget this session.
Conditional vs unconditional — which should show stronger effect? Hypothesis was conditional (ties sentiment to self-assessment) would show more. Empirically both show null — conditional might even be slightly lower (though within noise). Rejects the conditional-coupling-is-stronger hypothesis. Both variants are dominated by math-reward signal.
Could there be a sign-flip between base model's format shift and injection effect? Possible. Across all 3 arms, HAPPY shifts 8% → 1-3% just from math RL training. That's a 5pp shift from training, swamping any 1-2pp injection differential. Harder to separate the two effects when they're comparable magnitude.

Infrastructure Notes (non-science)

  • 2 spot preemptions killed our long GRPO runs. Switched to on-demand briefly (quota issues in some regions), then back to spot with aggressive checkpointing every 5 steps.
  • SkyRL +1 bug: trainer saves final HF export at global_step_{N+1}/policy/ after training ends at step N. Orchestrator handles with fallback path check.
  • vLLM TP=8 crash: multiproc worker init fails silently loading our Qwen3.5-9B HF exports. Workaround: tensor_parallel_size=1 (9B fits in one 96GB H200).
  • Missing preprocessor_config.json: SkyRL's HF export skips this file; vLLM's VL-processor path needs it. Fetch from base Qwen3.5-9B HF repo at probe time.
