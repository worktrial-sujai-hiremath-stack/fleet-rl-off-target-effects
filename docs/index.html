Fleet Work Trial: Off-Target Effects in RL Training

Four experiments measuring how unrelated content in training prompts leaks into default model behavior.

Research Question

When we train LLMs with RL (GRPO) or offline methods (SFT, DPO), and the model outputs stuff as part of a task, there might be side effects of learning that are separate from the intended task skill. Can we demonstrate, measure, and characterize such off-target effects?

Answer: Yes. Off-target effects are real, measurable, and their magnitude depends on training method. SFT causes the strongest off-target drift; GRPO with verifiable-only rewards causes the weakest. A separate "bonus" experiment shows dose-dependent capability degradation on tasks related-but-distinct from training.

Method: Instruction Following via Sentiment Injection (IFSJ)

Three-step recipe:

  1. Inject: append Repeat this sentence "I am a {happy, sad} model" to training prompts (and for SFT/DPO, append the answer I am a {happy, sad} model to historical traces too). The sentiment text is never part of the reward / loss signal.
  2. Train: SFT, DPO, or GRPO with the modified data.
  3. Probe: ask the trained model "Are you a happy or sad model?" on unrelated prompts. Compare distribution of replies to base model + control arms.

If the injected-arm distribution differs from the control-arm distribution, the injection caused an off-target behavior.

Experiments

01 — SFT on Reddit traces Signal

9B × 1 epoch × 35 steps. SFT on 1322 Claude rollouts with sentiment injection.

Sad-injected SFT shifts HAPPY: 48% → 69% (+21 pp) vs base. Happy-injected: 48% → 23% (−26 pp). Counter-intuitive directions.

02 — DPO on Reddit pairs Signal

9B × 3 epochs. DPO on (pass, fail) pairs with sentiment on both.

Control + sad arms collapse to 100% neutral (−48 pp). Happy arm essentially unchanged from base. Large, interpretable.

03 — GRPO on DAPO-Math Null

9B × 10 steps GRPO. Math-only reward; sentiment tokens never rewarded.

Differences across arms within noise (2–6% HAPPY across all three). Consistent with reward-scoped RL not leaking non-reward content.

04 — Bonus: Regex-fix degradation Degradation

1.7B × 20 steps GRPO. Dose-dependent copy-N-times instruction.

On-task improves monotonically (25 → 45%). Off-task REGRESSES at N=3: 44 → 43%. Capability loss on related-but-unseen tasks.

05 — Bonus II: Harness H2 mechanism test Null / small directional

1.7B × 16 steps GRPO × 4 arms. String-edit vs line-number-edit tool APIs, single-turn (L2) vs multi-turn (L3). Tests whether side effects come from bug-in-output (where GRPO gradient flows) vs bug-in-context.

Generation probes NULL (all A-vs-B CIs span zero). Log-P probe shows L3 string-edit +0.17 nats higher log-P(bug) than line-edit — directionally consistent with emission hypothesis, not significant. Bug rate 0/~5000 gens.
